## ⚡ Overview

**NO SMOKE** is a web-based medical student initiative that approaches tobacco dependence through cognitive regulation, highlighting that smoking is often a motor reflex rather than a conscious choice. Designed with a sleek, dark aesthetic, the application prompts users to reconsider their relationship with smoking by highlighting scientific facts about craving duration and the illusion of stress relief.

---

## 🧠 Core Scientific Concepts

The landing page breaks down smoking addiction into three main pillars:

### 1. The Autopilot Reflex (Automaticity)
*   **Concept**: Smoking is often driven by automatic motor impulses triggered by external cues (e.g., drinking coffee, waiting, boredom) before cognitive awareness takes place.
*   **Citation**: *Piper et al. (2004)*

### 2. The Stress Relief Illusion (Maladaptive Coping)
*   **Concept**: Nicotine does not reduce stress; instead, it raises the baseline heart rate and stress level. The perceived "calmness" is merely the temporary relief of withdrawal symptoms.
*   **Citation**: *Parrott (1999); Wood & Griffiths (2009)*

### 3. Taking Back the Wheel (Delay Strategy)
*   **Concept**: Craving spikes typically last only **3 to 5 minutes**. By practicing cognitive regulation ("urge surfing"), the Prefrontal Cortex can override subconscious motor reflexes.
*   **Citation**: *Kober et al. (2010)*
